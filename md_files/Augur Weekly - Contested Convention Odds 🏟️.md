### How it happens

The path to a contested convention starts with an unusually crowded primary
field, with half a dozen candidates who have a legitimate chance at the
nomination.

If Bernie does not earn a majority of pledged delegates (1,991 of 3,979)
available to win the nomination on the first ballot at the July convention,
any one of the remaining candidates could still have a shot at nabbing the
party's nomination at its national convention, set for July 13-16 in
Milwaukee, Wisconsin.
